Place Value and Number Sense

Understanding Hundreds, Tens and Ones

Numbers are made up of digits, and each digit has a place value. In a three-digit number:

  • The first digit is the hundreds place
  • The second digit is the tens place
  • The third digit is the ones place

For example: In the number 245:

  • 2 is in the hundreds place (2 hundreds = 200)
  • 4 is in the tens place (4 tens = 40)
  • 5 is in the ones place (5 ones = 5)

So 245 = 200 + 40 + 5

Practice Questions

What is the value of the 7 in 573?

The 7 is in the tens place, so it represents 70.

Write 300 + 50 + 6 as a single number.

300 + 50 + 6 = 356

Addition and Subtraction

Two-Digit Addition with Regrouping

When adding two-digit numbers, sometimes we need to regroup (carry over) when the sum in a column is 10 or more.

Example: 47 + 36

  1. Add the ones: 7 + 6 = 13
  2. Write down the 3 and carry over the 1 to the tens place
  3. Add the tens: 4 + 3 + 1 (the carried over) = 8
  4. So 47 + 36 = 83

Two-Digit Subtraction with Regrouping

When subtracting two-digit numbers, we sometimes need to regroup (borrow) when the top digit is smaller than the bottom digit.

Example: 52 - 27

  1. Look at the ones place: 2 is smaller than 7, so we need to borrow from the tens place
  2. Take 1 from the 5 (tens place), making it 4, and give 10 to the ones place (2 becomes 12)
  3. Now subtract ones: 12 - 7 = 5
  4. Subtract tens: 4 - 2 = 2
  5. So 52 - 27 = 25

Multiplication and Division Concepts

Multiplication as Repeated Addition

Multiplication is a quicker way to add the same number multiple times.

For example: 4 × 3 means 4 groups of 3, which is 3 + 3 + 3 + 3 = 12

Division as Sharing Equally

Division is about sharing a total amount into equal groups.

For example: 12 ÷ 3 means sharing 12 items equally among 3 friends. Each friend gets 4 items.

Practice Questions

What is 5 × 4?

5 × 4 = 20 (because 4 + 4 + 4 + 4 + 4 = 20)

If 18 apples are shared equally among 6 children, how many does each child get?

18 ÷ 6 = 3 apples per child

Fractions

Understanding Halves, Quarters and Eighths

A fraction represents equal parts of a whole.

  • Half (½): 1 out of 2 equal parts
  • Quarter (¼): 1 out of 4 equal parts
  • Eighth (⅛): 1 out of 8 equal parts

Measurement

Length, Mass and Capacity

We measure different things using appropriate units:

  • Length: millimeters (mm), centimeters (cm), meters (m)
  • Mass: grams (g), kilograms (kg)
  • Capacity: milliliters (mL), liters (L)

Practice Questions

Would you measure the length of a pencil in centimeters or meters?

Centimeters (cm) - because a pencil is small

Would you measure the weight of a watermelon in grams or kilograms?

Kilograms (kg) - because a watermelon is heavy

Time and Money

Reading Analog Clocks

Analog clocks have hour and minute hands:

  • The short hand shows the hour
  • The long hand shows the minutes
  • When the minute hand points to 12, it's exactly that hour
  • When it points to 6, it's half past the hour

Australian Money

Australian coins and notes:

  • Coins: 5¢, 10¢, 20¢, 50¢, $1, $2
  • Notes: $5, $10, $20, $50, $100

100 cents = $1
